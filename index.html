The Problem

CRM Engineered Around
Cognition. Not Emotion.

Aviation CRM is the most successful human factors intervention in history — accident rates dropped by over 50% following its introduction. It works because it engineers around known failure modes with trained skills and structured protocols.

But CRM was built around the cognitive axis only. Emotional failures remained invisible to the operator — the hazardous attitude, the dysregulated crew, the trust fracture that silenced the first officer. The emotional system had no engineering language, no failure mode taxonomy, no correction protocol.

ROTATE builds the missing axis. It treats emotion and cognition as coequal load-bearing structures of human performance — both subject to the same engineering principles that made CRM work.

The Gap
Emotional failures preempt system viability before cognitive skills can engage
The Insight
Emotion and cognition are coequal — neither is a product of the other
The Solution
A dual-axis control architecture with defined failure modes, skills, and correction protocols
The Reframe

Core Architecture

Three Foundational Claims

01
Co-Equality
Emotion and cognition are coequal engineering variables in Human-in-the-Loop systems. Neither produces the other. Both are modeled using five variables — X, Y, R, M, θ — and a coupling law that predicts their interaction. The system fails when either axis is ignored.
02
Mechanistic Frame
ROTATE reframes human interaction as mechanistic — naturally stripping morality from the construct. Failure is not a character flaw. It is a predictable output of a loaded system. This undercuts the primary barrier to growth: shame.
03
Engineerable
If failure modes are identifiable, they are predictable. If predictable, they are engineerable. ROTATE applies this principle to human systems — defining vulnerabilities, failure modes, trained skills, and structured correction protocols.
